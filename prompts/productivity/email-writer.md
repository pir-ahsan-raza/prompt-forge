# Write Emails That Don't Sound Like Emails

**Use when:** You need to write something professional but you don't want it to sound like a corporate memo — and you definitely don't want to spend 20 minutes on it.

---

## The Prompt

```
Write an email for me. 

Situation: [what happened / what you need / context]
Recipient: [who they are and your relationship — colleague, client, manager, stranger]
Goal: [what you want to happen after they read it]
Tone: [pick one: direct, warm, firm, apologetic, casual, formal]
Length: [short (under 100 words) / medium / as long as needed]

Rules for this email:
- Don't open with "I hope this email finds you well" or any version of that
- Don't close with "Please don't hesitate to reach out" 
- One ask per email — if there are multiple things I need, tell me so I can prioritize
- No passive-aggressive softening ("just wanted to check in..." / "circling back..." / "per my last email...")
- Say the main thing in the first two sentences

Write one version. Don't offer alternatives unless the situation genuinely has different angles worth considering.
```

---

## Common scenarios with pre-filled context

**Following up on something overdue:**
```
Situation: [person] hasn't responded to my email from [X days ago] about [topic]. This is time-sensitive.
Recipient: [relationship]
Goal: Get a response or at least know what's happening
Tone: Firm but not hostile
Length: Short — 3-4 sentences max
```

**Pushing back on a decision:**
```
Situation: My team/manager decided [X] and I disagree because [your reasoning]
Recipient: [manager / team lead]
Goal: Make my case clearly without burning goodwill
Tone: Direct and respectful
```

**Saying no to a request:**
```
Situation: [Someone] asked me to [do X] and I can't or won't, because [reason]
Recipient: [relationship]
Goal: Decline clearly without leaving the door open if I mean it's closed
Tone: [Warm / Direct] — delete one
```

---

## Notes

The "no alternatives" rule is important. If you ask for options you'll spend time choosing instead of sending. Make a decision on tone first, then generate one email.

The phrase blacklist in the main prompt is non-negotiable. These phrases are a reflex in AI-generated emails and they make every email sound the same.
